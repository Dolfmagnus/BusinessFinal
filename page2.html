Our Process

We Don't Mine.
We Reclaim.

Every piece of Terra & Thread jewelry starts as scrap — salvaged metal that would have ended up in a landfill. We refine, cast, and polish it in our East Lansing studio using zero-emission energy.

01

Collect

We source scrap silver, brass, copper, and gold from local jewelers, electronics recyclers, and industrial partners in Michigan.

→
02

Refine

Metal is sorted, cleaned, and smelted in our studio. Impurities are removed. The result is pure, beautiful raw material with zero new mining.

→
03

Craft

Our student artisans cast, shape, and hand-finish each piece. Every detail is deliberate. Every piece is unique and slightly one-of-a-kind.

→
04

Ship

Orders are packed in 100% recycled and compostable kraft materials. Seed-paper cards included with every order — plant them when you're done.

0 lbs New ore mined ever
2,400+ lbs metal reclaimed
100% Renewable studio energy
∞ Recyclable again after use

Our Story

Started in a
Dorm Room

Terra & Thread started when a pair of MSU students realized two things: the campus bookstore was overcharging for school-spirit jewelry, and most of that jewelry came from environmentally destructive supply chains.

So they started melting scrap metal in a shared studio space and making their own. What began as a side project for Greek life became a small, growing brand beloved by students, Greek chapters, and alumni alike.

Today we operate a zero-waste studio, work with five campus chapters, and have sold to Spartans in 22 states. We're still small, still student-run, and still deeply committed to the MSU community and the planet.

"

We believe sustainability and school spirit aren't opposites — they're two sides of the same Spartan coin.

— Founders, Terra & Thread
